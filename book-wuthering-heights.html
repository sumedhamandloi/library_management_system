Wuthering Heights

Emily Brontë

Wuthering Heights is an 1847 novel by Emily Brontë, initially published under her pen name "Ellis Bell". It concerns two families of the landed gentry living on the West Yorkshire moors, the Earnshaws and the Lintons, and their turbulent relationships with Earnshaw's adopted son, Heathcliff.

The novel was influenced by Romanticism and Gothic fiction. Wuthering Heights is now widely considered to be one of the greatest novels ever written in English, and it has inspired adaptations, including film, radio and television dramatizations, a musical by Bernard J. Taylor, a ballet, operas, a role-playing game, and a song by Kate Bush.
