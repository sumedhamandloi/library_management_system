Lord of the Flies

William Golding

Lord of the Flies is a 1954 novel by the Nobel Prize-winning British author William Golding. The plot concerns a group of British boys who are stranded on an uninhabited island and their disastrous attempts to govern themselves.

The novel explores the dark side of human nature and the conflict between civilization and savagery. Themes include the tension between groupthink and individuality, between rational and emotional reactions, and between morality and immorality.
