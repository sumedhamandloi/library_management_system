The Catcher in the Rye

J.D. Salinger

The Catcher in the Rye is a novel by J. D. Salinger, partially published in serial form in 1945-1946 and as a novel in 1951. Originally intended for adults, it is often read by adolescents for its themes of angst, alienation, and as a critique on superficiality in society.

The novel's protagonist and antihero, Holden Caulfield, has become an icon for teenage rebellion and angst. The story follows Holden's experiences in New York City in the days following his expulsion from Pencey Prep, a fictional University preparatory school in Pennsylvania.
