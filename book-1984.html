1984

George Orwell

Nineteen Eighty-Four is a dystopian social science fiction novel and cautionary tale written by English writer George Orwell. It was published on 8 June 1949 by Secker & Warburg as Orwell's ninth and final book completed in his lifetime.

The story takes place in an imagined future in the year 1984, when much of the world has fallen victim to perpetual war, omnipresent government surveillance, historical negationism, and propaganda. The novel follows Winston Smith, a low-ranking member of "the Party" who works for the Ministry of Truth, rewriting history to suit the Party's narrative.
